About Us

Motlalerain Youth Development Academy was established in 2022 February 1... The Founder of the Academy/ Organization Is Mr Itumeleng Gladwin Beng. Motlalerain Youth Development Academy is an established organisation registered as Non-Profit Organization under companies Act 71 of 2008.

The strategic objectives of the organisation among others are as follows: NB Assist learners with extra classes from grade. Providing an environment in which young people can meet to get involved in different artistic activities. Enable young people to train for and compete in competitive events, Organizing social events to benefit the Youth Academy and encourage an atmosphere in which young people of similar ideas and interest can meet to discuss any aspect of Art. Helping young people to improve their skills.

Registration

check more information about what we offer

We offer Extra classes from Grade 1 up to Grade 11. For learners from Grade 1 up to Grade 7, our focus is on helping them to read so that they can master their subjects. Additionally, we assist them with subjects they find challenging, as well as their homework and projects or assignments. For learners from Grade 8 up to Grade 11, we also provide support with subjects they may be struggling with.

We offer a platform for learners to showcase their talents. Classes are held every Saturday from 8am till 5pm. There is a once-off registration fee for the whole year, which is R150.

Kasi Dance & Rap Festive

During the third quarter, our organization hosted the vibrant "Kasi Dance & Rap Festive." This event provided a dynamic platform for youth to showcase their talents in dance and rap. With exciting prizes up for grabs, the event not only encouraged creative expression but also fostered a sense of community engagement among the youth. Through initiatives like these, we continue to support and empower our community members to explore their passions and talents.

Educational Fun Day

In the second quarter, our organization organized the highly successful "Educational Fun Day" on June 16th, 2023. This event brought together local schools, government departments, and visitors from North West University. Keynote speakers, including an Educational Psychologist, engaged with students and teachers, while representatives from entities such as the District Manager (Education), Department of Sports, Arts & Culture, Social Development, Municipality, and Tribal Office provided valuable insights and support.

Information Dissemination

Our focus during the first quarter was on hosting "Information Dissemination" events. These gatherings aimed to bring together various government departments with the goal of informing NPOs, NPCs, and NGOs about available opportunities across different sectors. Through these events, attendees gained valuable insights into funding opportunities, networking possibilities, and compliance requirements, thereby facilitating their ability to apply for funding and navigate the sector more effectively.
